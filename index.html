On Style

Most of the styling was copied or inspied from the Spartacus website. I wanted to give the site a little bit of a facelift, but still economical with my time. I thought a great place to look would be none other than the Spartacus Website Itself!

Search Text

Initially, I didn't realize that the textContent on a DomNode includes all descendants and did some research into the Treewalker API to traverse the dom and find text occurences. It ended up with way more occurences than were really there, because I was searching the same text multiple times. Even though it didn't pan out in the end, I'm still happy to have checked out an old browser api!

With Treewalker out of the way, I had some extra time to work on highlighting the searched word. In retrospect this turned out to be the hardest part of the project. (That's what I get for extra credit!) The difficulty here was because I needed to parse the raw html to add span tags and higlight the word or phrase. It turned out to be a ton of work to make sure I wasn't breaking the html when a word was searched that corresponped to an html tag. I tried a few different solutions to manually parse through the html, but I ended up using a regex in the end. It isn't perfect, but I decided to leave it in as is rather than nix the feature entirely. The highlighter also can't highlight across html tags. I think corrext way to do this in production would be to parse through the html yourself word by word, or to utilize a library.

Image Rotator

The image Rotator Was very straightfoward. I started by poking around to get some inspiration, then I got to coding. I ended up going with a pretty long fade in, because I thought it suited the moodiness of the photos themselves best. One thing I did have an issue with was responsiveness and image height. I ended up playing with widths until I got something I was happy with at most sizes. Because I used CSS grid, the bottom text will get pushed out at sizes smaller than 400px. I chose to keep it that way because The image gets way to busy with all the text. It's not a bug, it's a feature!
